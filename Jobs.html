Ideal Jobs

Alessio’s ideal job:

 

Job/Role: Owner of technology business

My ideal job is to be able to run a technology based business specialized in websites and software building a handful of applications and websites each with their own user base able to cater to different needs from different users.

I find the idea of building and running a business appealing due to the sense of achievement and creative freedom allowing me to express my ideas and pursue ideas I am passionate about.

I also enjoy the process of building a brand from the idea to marketing and communicating my message to like-minded individuals and working with really talented and interesting people makes me feel very satisfied with my work.

Lastly this position entails the following:

- Develop and maintain an I.T strategy

- Ensure targeted budgets are met and financials remain on target and profitable

- Ensure uptime of all infrastructure and internal systems

- Work with various vendors and partners to collaborate and form strategic business partnerships

- Manage all aspects of HR including recruitment, training and coaching of staff

Description (skills, experience and qualifications):

In my opinion this position entails many things including the following skills:

- Finance: Paying bills, managing money and investments as well as forecasting for future events and organizing money accordingly to ensure everything is paid for to avoid debt.

- Stress management: I think this is an important aspect of business as difficult times will always come up and managing it is crucial to stay on top of issues and difficult situations.

- Marketing/sales: To be able to sell your product or service and communicate brand values to customers as well as maintain relationships.

- Industry knowledge: This role will require knowledge and experience of the technology/I.T industry including standard protocols, software and hardware used as well as challenges and opportunities in the industry required to always stay up-to-date.

Qualifications & courses that may help:

  1. Bachelor of marketing
  2. Bachelor of I.T
  3. Bookkeeping course
  4. Introduction to business management concepts (Course available within the Bachelor of I.T)

Experience I currently have:

- Some technical experience and ideas of the I.T industry

- Experience with online marketing

- Experience with working in customer support

Plan on how I will obtain the skills, qualifications and experience required for the position, building on those you have now.

In order to meet the required skills for this role I will need to broaden my experience of marketing which could include the following areas of study and or practical experiences:

- Customer acquisition and retention

- Sales funnels

- Paid advertising and social media marketing

- Complete the Introduction to business management course to get an improved insight to management practices including the above HR requirements.

In order to achieve these goals I would start by applying for the 2 courses:

- Introduction to business management

- Introduction to bookkeeping

My plan:

Follow some online courses and blogs to learn more about paid advertising and social media which can be applied on smaller test projects before eventually been utilised to their full extent.

- Discuss my objectives with a mentor which can help me to improve my processes and address any shortcomings I discover over time.

- Continue to meet new people and learn from books, blogs, forums and through professional networking events to ensure I am constantly improving my skills.

- Look at other short courses to improve my core skills such as cyber security, finance & human resources.

With the above steps I believe I can acquire the relevant skills to begin working in a starting role to further gain the relevant experiences before being able to eventually climb the corporate ladder to a management role and or eventually form my own business.

Industry data 

What are the job titles for your groups ideal jobs? How do each of these rank in terms of demand from employers?

Alessio: Owner of technology/software business

Anton: Graduate trader

Vignesh: Senior manager of cyber security

Based on all the Burning glass data all our jobs collectively had elements that were in high demand by employers however all of our specific job titles didn’t rank significantly high with the highest ranking job title been a cyber security analyst and software developer. Some of the elements we all shared however included some of the following elements:

- Networking & system administration

- Computer operator roles

- Business intelligence for working with data in order to make better decisions.

From your groups ideal jobs, you can identify a set of skills required for these jobs (we will refer to this as your groups required skill set). These can be divided into general skills (communication, problem solving, writing etc) and IT-specific skills (JavaScript, SQL, etc).

How do the IT-specific skills in your required skill set rank in terms of demand from employers?

Based on all the teams ideal jobs they rank moderately with some elements of skills been present whilst the majority of higher ranking skills weren’t present. Some of the skills that were present across all our jobs and highly ranked in this data set included the following:

- Microsoft Windows

- Building relationships

- Microsoft office

- Project management

Aside from the above IT-specific skills the vast majority of our skill sets relevant to our ideal jobs were outside this range.

How do the general skills in your required skill set rank in terms of demand from employers?

Based on all the general skills laid out in this data set our required skill sets for our ideal jobs ranked really high with the following skills been paramount to our chosen jobs:

- Problem solving

- Communication

- Organisational skills Teamwork/collaboration

All of these attributes are in high demand from employers in various industries and would be a large part of our work in numerous situations.

What are the three highest ranked IT-Specific skills which are not in your required skill set?

From the burning glass data the biggest IT-specific skill that was missing from all our ideal jobs and experiences was SQL for working with databases. The second highest IT-Specific skill based on the data set which was also missing from our skill sets was Javascript which none of the team was proficient in. Lastly, another highly ranked skill the team wasn’t experienced with was the SAP software environment used by many businesses for record management and human resources activities.

What are the three highest ranked general skills which are not in your required skill set?

In terms of general skills the three highest ranking skills that were lacking from all our individual profiles included the following:

- Analytics

- Mentoring

- Quality assurance and control

As a team we identified the above areas were we lack the experience with our current skill sets while the higher ranking skills were closely aligned with our ideal jobs and collectively we believe that we possess these skills.

Having looked at the Burning Glass data, has your opinion of your ideal job changed? Why or why not?

Upon analysis of the Burning Glass data our opinions of our ideal jobs mostly remained the same primarily due to the fact that these were areas we felt passionate about and had knowledge about. Other reasons for this consensus was due to the flexible and varied nature of I.T where a vast array of skills and technologies can be applied to different job roles and industries making our choices somewhat flexible to adapt as needed.
